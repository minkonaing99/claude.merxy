## Signals (running)
- **REGISTER GATES THE TASTE EVEN HARDER THAN DOMAIN.** All signals below (bold/dark/photo-forward wins, restrained loses) come from **brand-register landing pages**. The first **product-register** job (xpenses, 2026-07-10) inverted the "restrained loses" signal completely: for app/dashboard UI, calm + single accent + zero decoration IS the brief and won outright, no explore-lane bold alternative was ever in contention (Generator A `taste-skill` doesn't even fire for product register). Don't apply the food/landing bold-bias below to app UI, admin panels, or dashboards — check register first, domain second.
- **DOMAIN GATES THE TASTE — the food-era biases below do NOT generalize.** First dev-tool job: user picked the LIGHT, restrained, Stripe-precise lane over the dark maximal Linear lane. For technical/developer/B2B audiences favour precision, whitespace-as-luxury, a single accent color, thin editorial display, and a real product-UI mockup (code→docs panel) as the hero element — NOT warmth/photography/drama. Scope every food signal below to food/drink/lifestyle briefs.
- **When the brief names real brands, ground in their measured `DESIGN.md`** (awesome-design-md, 74 brands). Two opposite-temperature refs (Linear dark vs Stripe light) hand you two divergent lanes for free — cheap and accurate, beats inventing.
- **Dev-tool hero = a built UI mockup, not stock imagery.** A source→generated-docs split panel in HTML/CSS out-reads any photo for this audience. Syntax-highlight purple is domain-correct, NOT an AI-slop tell.
- **Motion dial ≤3 = IntersectionObserver, not GSAP.** For "subtle scroll reveals," native IO at zero bundle cost is right; reserve GSAP for choreographed pin/scrub/timeline.
- **The real constant (3/3 food jobs): expressive big display + italic accent word in a contrast color + REAL photography as the loudest element.** Temperature is negotiable — dark won twice (coffee glow / pizza char), and on the dairy job the user kept BOTH a warm-LIGHT editorial AND a dark maximal. Don't assume dark; assume warm + photo-forward + a dramatic display face with one italic accent word. Refine of the old "always darkest" read.
- **Says "calm/clean/family" but never picks the restrained lane. CONFIRMED 3/3** (coffee→EXPLORE, pizza→dark maximal, dairy→rejected the Swiss-clean lane though brief said "mountain-clean"). Discount stated restraint ~1 notch; ALWAYS keep ≥1 bold lane. State the warning up front so they can override with words.
- **Photography is non-negotiable for food/drink.** Verify image URLs render before judging (a broken/wrong photo sinks a good layout). Real pasture/product shots beat icons/illustration.
- Loves **expressive display**: Fraunces 300 (light serif) and Bricolage Grotesque 700/800 (organic) both won. Italic accent word every time.
- Comfortable with **gradients + glow + radial atmosphere**; never picks the gradient-banned restrained variant.
- Pill buttons + soft shadow read premium. Squared/sharp buttons (the Swiss lane) lost.
- **Will take TWO winners when torn** — don't force a single pick if two lanes both land; offer to ship both.
